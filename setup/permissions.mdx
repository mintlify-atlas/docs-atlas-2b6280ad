---
title: macOS Permissions
description: Configure Full Disk Access and AppleScript automation permissions on macOS.
---

The iMessage & Mail skill reads protected SQLite databases and controls Messages.app and Mail.app via AppleScript. Both permission types must be granted before the skill can function.

## Full Disk Access

Full Disk Access lets your terminal read the SQLite databases that macOS protects by default — including `chat.db` (iMessage history), the Mail envelope index, and the Contacts database.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Privacy & Security settings">
    Open **System Settings** and navigate to **Privacy & Security** > **Full Disk Access**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add your terminal app">
    Click the **+** button and add the terminal application you use with Claude Code. Common options include:

    - Terminal.app (`/System/Applications/Utilities/Terminal.app`)
    - iTerm2 (`/Applications/iTerm.app`)
    - Warp (`/Applications/Warp.app`)
    - Ghostty (`/Applications/Ghostty.app`)

    If you launch Claude Code from an IDE terminal (e.g., VS Code), add the IDE's executable instead.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Restart your terminal">
    Fully quit your terminal application and reopen it. macOS does not apply Full Disk Access to already-running processes.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## AppleScript Automation

Sending iMessages and emails requires AppleScript access to Messages.app and Mail.app. macOS handles this through its Automation permission system.

**On first send**, macOS will display a dialog asking whether to allow your terminal to control Messages.app or Mail.app. Click **Allow**. This prompt appears once per app (once for Messages, once for Mail) and will not appear again after you grant it.

<Warning>
  If you click **Don't Allow**, the skill's send functionality will fail silently — the send command will appear to complete but no message will be delivered. To re-enable it, go to **System Settings** > **Privacy & Security** > **Automation**, find your terminal app, and enable the toggles for **Messages** and **Mail**.
</Warning>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title='"Permission denied" error when querying a database'>
    This means Full Disk Access has not been granted to your terminal, or the grant was applied before you restarted the terminal.

    1. Confirm your terminal appears in **System Settings** > **Privacy & Security** > **Full Disk Access** with its toggle enabled.
    2. Fully quit and relaunch the terminal.
    3. Retry the query.

    If you use multiple terminal apps, make sure you have added the one that is currently running Claude Code.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Send command completes but no message is delivered">
    This indicates the Automation permission for Messages.app or Mail.app was denied.

    1. Open **System Settings** > **Privacy & Security** > **Automation**.
    2. Find your terminal app in the list.
    3. Enable the toggle next to **Messages** (for iMessage) and/or **Mail** (for email).
    4. Retry the send command.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
